Ranking the Top 5 Captains of Indian Cricket Team

5. Virat Kohli

 One of the best contemporary batsmen in the world, Kohli has also been a successful captain of the Indian cricket team. He took the team to a new level and raised the bar very high, coming to fitness. However, his captaincy has come under scrutiny in ICC tournaments, as despite having the best team combination, India has been knocked out in the semi finals and has lost 2 finals. In SENA tours also, India has faced drubbing quite a few times.

4. Rohit Sharma

 Again, one of the most consistent and destructive batsmen of the present era, he is also a very successful captain in both international cricket and IPL. Leading Mumbai Indians to 5 IPL titles justifies his glory. Since taking over full time captaincy duties from Kohli, India has faced success in the first few tours. However, it is too early to decide as the actual test of captaincy is SENA tours. A lot of assignment in the coming years is in store for him, with the ICC World T20 2022 to be held in Australia and ICC Cricket World Cup 2023 to follow, in India, and the World Test Championship, besides the tours and series.

3. Sourav Ganguly

When Indian cricket was going through one of its hardest phases, if not the hardest, plagued by the match fixing scandal and controversies, Sourav Ganguly, affectionately known as 'Dada', took charge of the bleeding team in 2000 and accepted the challenge to rebuild the devastated team. He channelized aggression to transform the Indian team from a bunch of talented performers to a collective fighting unit, which went on to win various test series against the strongest teams. The 2001 Australia tour of India and 2003-04 India tour of Australia are perfect examples of that. India also won the 2002 NatWest Series and reached the finals of the 2003 World Cup, also to mention that India were joint winners of the 2002 Champions Trophy with Sri Lanka. The future stars like Yuvraj, Sehwag, Dhoni, Zaheer and many more were groomed by 'Dada'.

2. Kapil Dev

 A weak team then, India had crashed out of the first round in both the preceding world cups, winning against only East Africa. The team was considered as underdogs, and were never expected to win, with West Indies and Australia in India's group. However, Kapil Dev kept the team motivated and energetic, and after a roller coaster ride in the group stage, India defeated West Indies in the final to win their first World Cup title in 1983, marking the beginning of the cricketing folklore in India.

1. MS Dhoni

 A destructive power hitter, MS Dhoni is regarded as one of the two best captains of cricketing history, alongside Australian rival captain Ricky Ponting. After taking over reins in 2007, he led an inexperienced young Indian team to victory in the inaugural World T20, which showed his faith in youngsters and his ability to back players and groom their careers. He ended India's 28 year wait by winning the 2011 World Cup final, defeating Sri Lanka in the finals. A steep and tricky chase, Dhoni played extremely well under pressure, keeping his calmness intact. He further went on to win the 2013 Champions Trophy, becoming the first and only captain till date to win all three ICC titles. The cricket superstars of today like Kohli, Rohit, Dhawan, Jadeja and Ashwin owe their career to Dhoni. He has left such an impact that some players have even accepted a lower price to play for Chennai Super Kings, the IPL franchise Dhoni leads, when other franchises offered them a higher price. Leading CSK to 4 IPL titles, he has also been instrumental in nurturing the careers of young foreign cricketers who played for the franchise.
